Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Borlaug was an American neurologist and psychoanalyst who was the director of the National Neurological Institute from 1985 to 1994. He is best known for his work with psychological disorders, particularly substance abuse and addiction. Borlaug was awarded the Nobel Prize in Psychology in 1994 for his work on addiction and was the first African-American to be awarded the Nobel Prize. He is survived by his wife, Mary Kay Borlaug, and their two children.
